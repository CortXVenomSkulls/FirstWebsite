Soundgarden was an American rock band formed in Seattle,
        Washington, in 1984 by singer and drummer Chris Cornell,
        lead guitarist Kim Thayil (both of whom are the only
        members to appear in every incarnation of the band), and
        bassist Hiro Yamamoto. Cornell switched to rhythm guitar in
        1985, replaced on drums initially by Scott Sundquist, and
        later by Matt Cameron in 1986. Yamamoto left in 1990 and
        was replaced initially by Jason Everman and shortly
        thereafter by Ben Shepherd. The band dissolved in 1997 and
        re-formed in 2010. Following Cornell's death in 2017 and a
        year of uncertainty regarding the band's future, Thayil
        declared in October 2018 that Soundgarden had disbanded
        once again, though they did reunite in January 2019 for a
        one-off concert in tribute to Cornell.
